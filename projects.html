How does organic matter affect methane cycling in anoxic marine sediment?

This work aims to understand the geochemical factors that determine if methane is biotically consumed or produced. We use cores of sediment to track environmental changes with depth and predict how organic matter lability and subsequent hydrogen production impacts methane cycling in both organic-rich and organic-poor marine systems. This includes microbial community analysis too, through 16S rRNA gene libraries and soon metagenomes.

How does temperature stress affect our soil's microbial communities and plant performance?

We are working on identifying changes in both microbial community composition and activity associated with increased soil temperature. We accompolish this by incubating soil for months and then selectively inhibiting bacteria or fungi to measure their respiration as a proxy for activity. Oak trees are grown in these soils and microbial communities are extracted and analyzed. My work is part of a larger project, Silva Nova.
